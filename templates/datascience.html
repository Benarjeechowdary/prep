Data Science Interview Questions

  • What is the difference between supervised and unsupervised learning?
    Supervised learning uses labeled data to train models, while unsupervised learning finds patterns and structures in unlabeled data.
  • Explain the concept of overfitting and how to prevent it.
    Overfitting occurs when a model performs well on training data but poorly on unseen data. Prevent it by using regularization, pruning, and early stopping.
  • What are the key assumptions of linear regression?
    Linearity, independence, homoscedasticity, normality of residuals, and lack of multicollinearity.
  • How do you handle missing data in a dataset?
    Use techniques like imputation (mean, median, or mode), removal of rows, or using models to predict missing values.
  • What is the curse of dimensionality in machine learning?
    The curse of dimensionality refers to the difficulty in modeling data as the number of features increases, leading to sparse data and overfitting.
  • Explain the difference between classification and regression.
    Classification predicts discrete labels, while regression predicts continuous values.
  • What are precision, recall, and F1-score?
    Precision = TP/(TP + FP), Recall = TP/(TP + FN), F1-score = 2 × (Precision × Recall) / (Precision + Recall).
  • How do you evaluate the performance of a machine learning model?
    Use metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, ROC-AUC, and confusion matrix.
  • What is cross-validation and why is it important?
    Cross-validation splits data into training and testing sets multiple times to evaluate model performance and prevent overfitting.
  • What is the difference between bagging and boosting?
    Bagging reduces variance by training models in parallel, while boosting reduces bias by training models sequentially.
  • Explain the working of a Random Forest algorithm.
    Random Forest creates multiple decision trees during training and outputs the mode (classification) or mean (regression) of the predictions.
  • What are some common feature engineering techniques?
    Scaling, encoding categorical variables, feature extraction, polynomial features, and missing value imputation.
  • How do you deal with imbalanced datasets?
    Use techniques like oversampling, undersampling, SMOTE, or adjusting class weights.
  • What is PCA (Principal Component Analysis) and when should you use it?
    PCA reduces the dimensionality of data while preserving variance. Use it to remove noise and improve model performance.
  • How does a convolutional neural network (CNN) work?
    A CNN uses convolutional layers to extract spatial features from images, followed by pooling and fully connected layers for classification.
